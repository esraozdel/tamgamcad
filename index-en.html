TamgamCAD helps you make 2D technical drawings with basic and advanced drawing tools. While straight line, continuous line, circle, ellipse, arc, and rectangle are included in the basic drawing tools, polygon, ring, TTR, and tangent line are among the advanced drawing tools. Apart from these, it offers a wide toolset that allows detailing drawings with vector-based freehand drawing tools and different brush types. Practicality is highly important during drawing; therefore, the tools located in the editing and operations sections bring speed to the user.


Editing Section Tools

  • Trim & Extend: Used to prune excess intersecting lines or to extend the end of a line to another object.
  • Offset: Creates a parallel copy of a selected object (for example, a wall line) to the outside or inside by a specified distance.
  • Rotate: Allows selected objects to be rotated around a specified center point by the entered angle value.
  • Mirror: Creates a mirror image of the selected objects around a specified reference line.
  • Scale: Used to proportionately enlarge or reduce objects based on a starting reference point.
  • Array: Multiplies a selected object at specific intervals and quantities on a horizontal and vertical plane (Rectangular) or around a circular center (Polar).
  • Chamfer & Fillet: Cuts the sharp corners formed by two intersecting lines straight by entering a specific distance (Chamfer) or creates a soft curve with a specific radius (Fillet).
  • Resize: Used to change the dimensions of drawn objects later.

Operations Section Tools

  • Group & Ungroup: You can use group to select multiple objects and instantly move them as a single object, or ungroup to make the components of an existing group independent again.
  • Explode & Join: Used to explode an entire polyline or polygon into independent lines, or to join open-ended, touching independent lines together as a single object.
  • Wrap / Boundary: Deletes the inner details of objects and creates a new, single-piece, and closed boundary geometry based only on their outermost borders.
  • Boolean Operations: Mathematically processes overlapping closed shapes.
    • Union: Melds overlapping shapes into a single whole.
    • Difference: Cuts and removes the areas from the main shape where the second shape touches or intersects.
    • Intersection: Leaves only the common (intersecting) area where the shapes overlap, and deletes the rest.
    • Exclusion / XOR: The exact opposite of the intersection operation; it leaves only the distinct parts of the shapes that do not touch each other and deletes the common area.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is an internet connection required to use TamgamCAD?

No, an internet connection is not mandatory. Thanks to its local workspace feature, TamgamCAD stores all your projects and drawings on your device, allowing you to work completely offline.

Can I use the Pro features I purchased on Google Play on the Microsoft Store?

Is it easy to use on tablets and touchscreens?

Absolutely! Our interface is specifically optimized for touchscreens. You can zoom/pan with two fingers and create drawings with millimeter precision using your stylus.
